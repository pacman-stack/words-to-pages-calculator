# How Many Pages Is 1,500 Words?

1,500 words is approximately **3 pages single-spaced** or **6 pages double-spaced** using 12pt Times New Roman with 1-inch margins on standard letter paper (8.5" × 11").

That's the quick answer. But page count depends heavily on four variables: font, font size, line spacing, and margins. Adjust any one of them and your numbers change.

---

## The Standard Academic Answer

When a professor assigns a "1,500-word essay," they typically expect:

- **Font:** Times New Roman or Arial
- **Size:** 12pt
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides

Under those conditions, 1,500 words fills roughly **5.5 to 6 pages**.

---

## How Font Affects Page Count

Font choice has the biggest impact on page length. Here's how 1,500 words compares across common fonts at 12pt, double-spaced:

| Font | Pages (double-spaced) |
|------|-----------------------|
| Times New Roman | ~6 pages |
| Arial | ~6.3 pages |
| Calibri | ~5.7 pages |
| Georgia | ~6.2 pages |
| Courier New | ~7.5 pages |

Courier New is an outlier — it's a monospace font designed to mimic typewritten text, so it takes up significantly more space. If your teacher hasn't specified a font, Times New Roman or Arial are the safe defaults.

---

## Single-Spaced vs. Double-Spaced: The Full Breakdown

| Spacing | Pages for 1,500 Words |
|---------|-----------------------|
| Single-spaced | ~3 pages |
| 1.5 spacing | ~4.5 pages |
| Double-spaced | ~6 pages |

Double-spacing is the standard for most academic writing in North America. It makes essays easier to mark up and read. If your assignment just says "essay," double-spaced at 12pt is a safe assumption.

---

## How Font Size Changes Everything

The same text at different font sizes:

| Font Size | Pages (double-spaced, Times New Roman) |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| 10pt | ~4.5 pages |
| 11pt | ~5 pages |
| 12pt | ~6 pages |
| 14pt | ~7.5 pages |

Going from 10pt to 14pt adds roughly 3 pages. This is why some students quietly bump their font from 12pt to 14pt — but most teachers notice.

---

## Margins Matter More Than You'd Think

Standard margins are 1 inch on all sides. Here's what happens when you change them:

| Margins | Pages for 1,500 Words (12pt, double-spaced) |
|---------|---------------------------------------------|
| 0.5 inch | ~4.5 pages |
| 1 inch (standard) | ~6 pages |
| 1.25 inch | ~6.8 pages |
| 1.5 inch | ~7.5 pages |

Wider margins mean more pages. If you're running short, widening margins is another trick students use — and again, most instructors can tell.

---

## What Does 1,500 Words Look Like?

To put it in context:

- A typical news article runs 600–900 words
- A short blog post is around 800–1,200 words
- A 1,500-word essay is a medium-length academic piece — roughly 6 double-spaced pages
- A typical book chapter is 3,000–5,000 words, so 1,500 words is about half a chapter

If you're typing it yourself: at an average typing speed of 40 words per minute, 1,500 words takes about **37 minutes** to write (not counting research or thinking time).

---

## Common Uses for 1,500-Word Assignments

**High school essays:** A 1,500-word essay is a standard length for history papers, English literature responses, or persuasive writing assignments. At 12pt, double-spaced, expect 5–6 pages.

**College papers:** In university writing, 1,500 words is on the shorter end — often used for response papers, case studies, or short analytical essays. Professors usually want a clear thesis, 2–3 supporting arguments, and a conclusion.

**Blog content:** In SEO writing, 1,500 words is considered a solid mid-length post. It's long enough to cover a topic thoroughly but short enough to keep readers engaged.

**Short reports:** A 1,500-word business or research report typically runs 3–4 pages single-spaced with section headers and some white space.
