Double spacing is the standard format for academic writing — essays, research papers, dissertations, and most assignments from high school through university.

---

## Double-Spaced Pages Quick Reference

All estimates use 12pt Times New Roman, 1-inch margins, standard letter paper:

| Word Count | Double-Spaced Pages |
|------------|---------------------|
| 250 words | ~1 page |
| 500 words | ~2 pages |
| 750 words | ~3 pages |
| 1,000 words | ~4 pages |
| 1,500 words | ~6 pages |
| 2,000 words | ~8 pages |
| 2,500 words | ~10 pages |
| 3,000 words | ~12 pages |
| 5,000 words | ~20 pages |
| 10,000 words | ~40 pages |

Rule of thumb: **250 words = 1 double-spaced page** at standard academic settings.

---

## What Exactly Is Double Spacing?

Double spacing sets the line height to twice the font size, leaving a blank line's worth of space between each line of text. In Microsoft Word and Google Docs, "double spacing" uses a line spacing value of 2.0.

## How Font Size Changes Your Page Count

| Font Size | Pages for 1,000 Words |
|-----------|----------------------|
| 10pt | ~3.1 pages |
| 11pt | ~3.5 pages |
| 12pt | ~4.0 pages |
| 14pt | ~5.0 pages |

APA 7th edition recommends 12pt Times New Roman or 11pt Calibri. MLA requires 12pt.

---

## Double Spacing vs. Single Spacing

- **1,000 words single-spaced** → ~2 pages
- **1,000 words double-spaced** → ~4 pages

If someone asks for "4 pages double-spaced," they're asking for roughly 1,000 words.

---

## How to Set Double Spacing

**Microsoft Word:** Select all (Ctrl+A) → Home → Line Spacing → 2.0

**Google Docs:** Format → Line & paragraph spacing → Double
