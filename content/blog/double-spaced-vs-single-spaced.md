Line spacing is one of the most important formatting decisions you'll make when writing a document. Whether you're writing a college essay, a business report, or a novel manuscript, choosing the right spacing affects readability, page count, and whether your document meets submission requirements.

## What Is Line Spacing?

Line spacing (also called leading) refers to the vertical distance between lines of text. The most common options are:

- **Single-spaced (1.0)** — Lines are close together with minimal gap
- **1.15 spacing** — Slightly more than single, the default in Google Docs
- **1.5 spacing** — A middle ground often used in European academic writing
- **Double-spaced (2.0)** — A full blank line between each line of text

## How Spacing Affects Page Count

The difference is dramatic. For a 1,000-word document in 12pt Times New Roman:

| Spacing | Approximate Pages |
|---------|------------------|
| Single (1.0) | ~2 pages |
| 1.15 | ~2.3 pages |
| 1.5 | ~3 pages |
| Double (2.0) | ~4 pages |

That means switching from single to double spacing roughly **doubles your page count**.

## When to Use Double Spacing

Double spacing is standard in these contexts:

### Academic Writing
- **APA format** requires double spacing throughout
- **MLA format** requires double spacing throughout
- **Chicago/Turabian** uses double spacing for the main text
- Most college essays and research papers expect double spacing
- Dissertations and theses typically require double spacing

### Why Professors Require It
Double spacing isn't just tradition — it serves practical purposes:
- Leaves room for handwritten feedback and corrections
- Improves readability for grading long papers
- Makes it easier to estimate word count at a glance
- Provides visual breathing room for dense academic content

## When to Use Single Spacing

Single spacing is appropriate for:

### Business Documents
- Business letters and memos
- Internal reports and briefs
- Email communications
- Resumes (though 1.15 is often better)

### Published Content
- Books and ebooks
- Magazine and newspaper articles
- Website content and blog posts
- Marketing materials

### Personal Writing
- Notes and outlines
- Journal entries
- Draft manuscripts (some writers prefer this for editing)

## The 1.15 and 1.5 Compromise

### 1.15 Spacing
Google Docs uses 1.15 as its default spacing. It's slightly more readable than single spacing without the significant page increase of double spacing. Many modern business documents use this setting.

### 1.5 Spacing
Common in European universities and some professional documents. It provides good readability without using as much paper as double spacing. Some style guides specifically recommend 1.5 spacing.

## How to Change Line Spacing

### Microsoft Word
1. Select all text (Ctrl+A)
2. Go to Home tab → Line Spacing button
3. Choose your spacing option

### Google Docs
1. Select all text (Ctrl+A)
2. Go to Format → Line & paragraph spacing
3. Choose single, 1.15, 1.5, or double

### Pages (Mac)
1. Select your text
2. Click the Format button
3. Adjust the spacing value under "Spacing"
