# Words to Pages Double Spaced: The Complete Guide

Double spacing is the standard format for academic writing — essays, research papers, dissertations, and most assignments from high school through university. If you've ever wondered how many pages your word count translates to in double-spaced format, here's everything you need to know.

---

## Double-Spaced Pages Quick Reference

All estimates below use 12pt Times New Roman, 1-inch margins, standard letter paper (8.5" × 11"):

| Word Count | Double-Spaced Pages |
|------------|---------------------|
| 250 words | ~1 page |
| 500 words | ~2 pages |
| 750 words | ~3 pages |
| 1,000 words | ~4 pages |
| 1,500 words | ~6 pages |
| 2,000 words | ~8 pages |
| 2,500 words | ~10 pages |
| 3,000 words | ~12 pages |
| 5,000 words | ~20 pages |
| 10,000 words | ~40 pages |

A simple rule of thumb: **250 words = 1 double-spaced page** at standard academic settings.

---

## What Exactly Is Double Spacing?

Double spacing sets the line height to twice the font size, leaving a blank line's worth of space between each line of text. In Microsoft Word and Google Docs, "double spacing" uses a line spacing value of 2.0.

It exists for a practical reason: it gives instructors and editors room to write feedback, corrections, and comments between lines of text. It's not a trick to make papers look longer — though it does have that effect.

---

## How Font Changes Your Double-Spaced Page Count

Switching fonts can add or subtract a full page from a long essay. Here's how common fonts compare at 12pt, double-spaced, for 1,000 words:

| Font | Pages for 1,000 Words |
|------|-----------------------|
| Times New Roman | ~4.0 pages |
| Arial | ~4.2 pages |
| Calibri | ~3.8 pages |
| Courier New | ~4.5 pages |
| Georgia | ~4.1 pages |
| Garamond | ~3.7 pages |

**Times New Roman** is the academic default for a reason — it's compact but highly readable. **Courier New** runs significantly larger because every character occupies the same horizontal space (monospace design).

---

## How Font Size Changes Your Double-Spaced Page Count

| Font Size | Pages for 1,000 Words (Double-Spaced) |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|
| 10pt | ~3.1 pages |
| 11pt | ~3.5 pages |
| 12pt | ~4.0 pages |
| 13pt | ~4.5 pages |
| 14pt | ~5.0 pages |

Most style guides specify 12pt. APA 7th edition recommends 12pt Times New Roman or 11pt Calibri. MLA requires 12pt. Chicago style typically uses 12pt as well.

---

## Double Spacing vs. Single Spacing: The Difference

Double spacing produces exactly twice the pages of single spacing — in theory. In practice it's slightly less than double because paragraph spacing and heading formatting behave differently. But for body text:

- **1,000 words single-spaced** → ~2 pages
- **1,000 words double-spaced** → ~4 pages

If someone asks you to "write 4 pages double-spaced," they're asking for roughly 1,000 words of content.

## How to Set Double Spacing in Common Tools

**Microsoft Word:**
Select all text (Ctrl+A) → Home tab → Line Spacing → 2.0

**Google Docs:**
Format → Line & paragraph spacing → Double

**LibreOffice Writer:**
Format → Paragraph → Line Spacing → Double
