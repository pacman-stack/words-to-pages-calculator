# How Many Pages Is 1,000 Words?

1,000 words is approximately **2 pages single-spaced** or **4 pages double-spaced** using 12pt Times New Roman with 1-inch margins on standard letter paper (8.5" × 11").

That's the short answer. The real answer depends on four variables: font, font size, line spacing, and margins. Change any one of them and your page count shifts.

---

## The Standard Academic Answer

Most teachers and professors mean this when they assign a "1,000-word essay":

- **Font:** Times New Roman or Arial
- **Size:** 12pt
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides

Under those conditions, 1,000 words fills roughly **3.5 to 4 pages**.

---

## How Font Affects Page Count

Font choice is the single biggest variable. Here's how 1,000 words compares across common fonts at 12pt, double-spaced:

| Font | Pages (double-spaced) |
|------|-----------------------|
| Times New Roman | ~4 pages |
| Arial | ~4.2 pages |
| Calibri | ~3.8 pages |
| Courier New | ~4.5 pages |
| Georgia | ~4.1 pages |

Courier New runs larger because it's a monospace font — every character takes up the same width, including narrow letters like "i" and "l." Times New Roman is more compact. The difference between fonts can be half a page or more on a 1,000-word document.

---

## How Font Size Affects Page Count

Font size has a near-linear effect on page count:

| Font Size | Pages (double-spaced, Times New Roman) |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| 10pt | ~3.1 pages |
| 11pt | ~3.5 pages |
| 12pt | ~4 pages |
| 14pt | ~5 pages |

Most academic papers require 11pt or 12pt. Some style guides (like APA 7th edition) default to 12pt. If you're writing for a class, always check the rubric before changing the font size.

---

## How Line Spacing Affects Page Count

This is the most common way students stretch or shrink a paper:

| Spacing | Pages (12pt Times New Roman) |
|---------|------------------------------|
| Single-spaced | ~2 pages |
| 1.5-spaced | ~3 pages |
| Double-spaced | ~4 pages |

Double-spacing is standard for academic work. It exists to give instructors room to write comments between lines — not to help students hit a page count.

---

## Quick Reference: 1,000 Words by Use Case

| Use Case | Typical Format | Pages |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| College essay | 12pt, double-spaced | ~4 |
| Blog post (published) | 12pt, single-spaced | ~2 |
| Novel manuscript | 12pt, double-spaced | ~4 |
| Business report | 11pt, single-spaced | ~1.8 |
| Resume | 11pt, single-spaced | ~1.7 |

---

## Why Word Count Matters More Than Page Count

Pages are a misleading measure of effort. A 4-page double-spaced essay contains the same amount of content as a 2-page single-spaced essay. Publishers, editors, and most professional writing contexts use word count — not pages — as the standard unit.

If someone asks for "4 pages double-spaced," they're really asking for ~1,000 words. If they ask for "2 pages single-spaced," same thing.

## How to Count Your Words

- **Microsoft Word:** Bottom status bar shows word count, or go to Review → Word Count
- **Google Docs:** Tools → Word Count (Ctrl+Shift+C / Cmd+Shift+C)
- **Plain text:** Paste into [wordtopages.com](https://wordtopages.com) to get both word count and page estimate instantly
